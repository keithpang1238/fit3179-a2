The prominent rows of the heatmap show that there are around six top causes of death that are common across many countries. These are examined further in the per-country section below.
Death statistics for Australia
Select Country:
Most countries have the most deaths in the 85+ and 80-84 age ranges. However, some developing countries, like India and Indonesia, have more deaths in younger age ranges, as well as relatively high deaths in the under one year old range due to neonatal conditions.
Deaths resulting from most of these most significant cause has decreased or remained stable over time for most countries. However, Dementia has consistently become a more prevalent cause of death, particularly for developed countries like Australia and the USA. Developing countries have also seen Ischemic Heart Disease rising in prevalence over time.
